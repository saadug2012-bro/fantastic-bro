Basic Working Process

  1. Internet comes from ISP to router
  2. Router sends data using radio waves
  3. Devices receive and send data wirelessly

Wi‑Fi works by using radio waves to send and receive data between devices and a wireless router. The router is connected to the internet through an Internet Service Provider (ISP). When a user opens a website or sends data from their phone or laptop, the device sends signals to the router using radio frequencies. The router then communicates with the internet and sends the data back to the device. Most Wi‑Fi networks operate on 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz frequency bands. The 2.4 GHz band covers longer distances but is slower, while the 5 GHz band is faster but has a shorter range. Modern Wi‑Fi standards such as Wi‑Fi 6 are used in the UAE to provide faster speeds, better security, and support for many connected devices
